What is the Difference Between Anxiety and Depression?

What is anxiety?

  • Apprehension over what's about to happen and what could happen in the future
  • Worried thoughts, or a belief that something could go wrong
  • Feeling like you need to run away or avoid things that could cause further anxiety

What is depression?

  • Feeling of sadness about the future, as though it's hopeless
  • Lack of belief that positive things will occur
  • Little worry, but instead a certainty of future negative emotions. Possible suicidal thoughts

What is social anxiety?

  • The defining feature of social anxiety disorder, also called social phobia, is intense anxiety or fear of being judged, negatively evaluated, or rejected in a social or performance situation.
  • Introversion does not equal social anxiety

Bad news: anxiety and depression are not curable; they can be maintained and balanced with the right medication and treatment

Physical Issues: Sitting Too Much

  • Organ damage to heart, pancreas, colon
  • Digestion issues and brain damage
  • Poor posture
  • Pain in neck (heavy backpacks can cause this too!), upper back, lower back, hips, hamstrings, glutes, and thighs

Unexpected Effects of Screen Time

  • Strained, dry eyes, blurred vision, and headaches
  • Blue light from digital devices suppresses the sleep-promoting hormone melatonin, keeping us from having restful sleep

How Sunlight Affects Your Mental Health

  • Shorter days and less sunlight lead to depression
  • Lack of vitamin D can lead to depression
  • Serotonin is also released in response to sunlight, which helps elevate your mood and energy

Social anxiety can occur at...

  • Work
  • Attending meet-ups, conferences, social events, etc.
  • Tech interviews/tech tests

Events, meet-ups, conferences, etc.

  • Imposter syndrome kicks in
  • Crowds can scare people
  • Finding it uncomfortable to engage in coversation/meeting new people

Tech interviews/tech tests

  • Live coding/unrealistic scenarios for tech testing
  • Finding it hard to converse
  • Feeling intimidated or overwhelmed by meeting new people

Work

  • No one at work will like me
  • Feeling left out
  • I won't make any friends
  • I will be judged negatively for my skills
  • Feeling like there is an expectation at work that you have to be outgoing and extroverted all the time
  • Introverted people/people with social anxiety can come across "cold" or "uninterested", but does not mean they're not kind or friendly
  • It's okay to have "off days"; some days you don't want to converse that much (make sure you are still professional in meetings, for example, and are able to do your work)
  • Getting along with your co-workers; they can have a large impact on your job experience and health
  • If you work with toxic co-workers, you will likely find your social anxiety to increase and/or your mental health to suffer

5. Tips && advice for mental + physical well-being

  • Try not to compare yourself to others
  • Be patient with yourself: you're only human!
  • Worrying is normal: try to dedicate a few moments during the day to worry
  • Write worries down on a piece of paper
  • There will always be jobs available! Be patient, and the right one will come along (I know, cliché, right?)
  • If you feel overwhelmed by web development, find interests outside of coding
  • Try to work on small tasks, leading up to a larger project in the end
  • Code a little bit every day or a few days a week, outside of work
  • Mentor and volunteer — teaching others helps you learn
  • Find a mentor to help you!
  • Go to an in-person workshop
  • Make time to spend with family and friends
  • ASK FOR HELP — coding problems, financial support, emotional support, etc.
  • At work, take a small break every ~20mins; walk around and/or stretch
  • If you're struggling with a problem, walk away from the computer
  • Clean your space at home and at work
  • Coming home to a messy and dirty home can increase mental unrest
  • Go on vacations
  • Take personal/sick days

Limit time spent on social media, news sites and TV, etc.

Offer your time donate or volunteer for a cause you care about

Summary

Looking out for your well-being both mentally and physically is more important than any job you will have. If your health suffers, your work suffers. If your workplace is toxic, it can have serious effects on your health. It's crucial to find a happy balance of a good workplace and good health. No job is perfect, but there is no point in suffering.
